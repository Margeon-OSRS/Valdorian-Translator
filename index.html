Rules

Valdorian (Vethrin, "the spoken") is the lingua franca of the Confederation, carried across Valdoria, Varn, and Charskin Isle. It is built to be transparent: roots join at the seam, and every grammatical change is a single, visible affix.

Sound

The tongue favours liquids and soft fricatives. Its signatures are the digraphs th /θ/, dh /ð/, lh /)/ (a palatal l), and rh /ʁ/, with clustered onsets like vr-, thr-, dr-, and mir-. It has no c, q, x, j, nor the sounds sh, zh, ts. Vowels are a e i o u; the diphthongs ae /æ/, ai, ei, oi.

Word order

Subject – verb – object, as in English, so the lexicon reads straight across. Adjectives and the genitive follow their noun.

Tense is a suffix

A verb's time is carried on its tail. The present is bare.

Most verbs end in -ir, the active class; a smaller set in -an, the class of states and feelings (vaelan to love). Tense attaches after the class ending.

Number

Two plurals, split by how a thing is counted. A count plural takes the suffix -ai (-i after a vowel):

andel friend → andelai friends

A collective (a whole people or body taken as one) takes the prefix vel-:

valan person → velvalan the people

Genitive & negation

"Of" turns a noun into a modifier with the suffix -en: goleth gold → golethen golden / of gold. Many adjectives are frozen genitives, which is why so many end in -en.

Negation is the prefix nav- (na- before a v): soren true → navsoren untrue.

Register on pronouns

A pronoun shows how the speaker stands to the one addressed. The neutral form is bare; the exalted prefix vae- is used toward a sovereign, the Senate, or the record; the familiar prefix lo- is used downward or between intimates.

Compounding

The pride of the tongue. Roots join with no glue, and the meaning is the sum:

mira water · vald land → miravald island

A few seeds recur as endings: -ath (a great body or institution), -ar (grand), -an (the one who bears or does), -el (a vessel), arn / orn (one who rules).

morren council → morrenath senate · soluth pact → soluthar confederation

A line, all at once.
Velvalan vaelanor sa soluthar.
"The people loved the confederation." Built from collective vel-, past -or, the article sa, and the grand compound soluthar.

Rural Vethrin

The unhurried speech of Valdoria's southern reaches. Not a tongue apart, but standard Vethrin softened in the mouth — clusters loosen, hard sounds give way, and the last vowel is drawn long. It borrows every word from the standard lexicon and bends the sound, so nothing here needs its own dictionary.

One tongue, slower. Rural Vethrin keeps the grammar of the standard speech — the same word order, the same suffixes for tense and number, the same prefixes for the collective and negation. Only the sound relaxes. A speaker of the council halls and a speaker of the southern farms understand one another without trouble; the difference is in the ease of the mouth, not the meaning.

The whole lexicon, spoken the southern way. Every standard word with the rural shifts laid over it — the standard form is noted beneath each, since the two share one meaning.
